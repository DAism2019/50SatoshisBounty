50Satoshis Bounty

The Satoshi UTO Fund was forged by 50 anonymous participants, each contributing 0.02 ETH. To commemorate the successful establishment of the Satoshi UTO Fund, recognize the contributions of these 50 Satoshis, and to permanently preserve the reward model of the public fund designed by Satoshi Nakamoto in Bitcoin's Proof of Work (PoW) system (which could not continue operating for the next 16 years due to security issues), we are replicating the reward rules of Bitcoin's PoW in the name of Satoshi Nakamoto.
  • The total prize pool is 21,000,000 UTO, which is equivalent to 21,000,000 jeedd (price unit).
  • A reward is given for each block produced, similar to the PoW rewards of Bitcoin, and is paid out per block.
  • The total reward for the first block is 50 UTO. This reward is evenly distributed among 50 anonymous participants (wallet addresses), so each wallet address receives a reward of 1 UTO for the first block.
  • Following the Bitcoin halving model, the reward is halved every 210,000 blocks.
  • In imitation of Bitcoin's halving every four years, there will be a total of four halvings, celebrating the 16th anniversary of Bitcoin's inception on January 3, 2025!
  • The 50 anonymous participants can see real-time prize flows in their wallets (technically, Bitcoin's reward mechanism is a perfectly flawless Money Streaming that is severely underestimated across the industry).
  • Any participant can withdraw their prize at any time.

SUF Development Event: SufAIthon

Event Overview

As the name suggests, SufAIthon is a development activity built on an AI-constructed knowledge system that enables auditing and decision-making capabilities. It leverages smart contracts to provide self-executing abilities resilient to human interference, supported by funding from the Satoshi UTO Fund, aiming to offer solutions for human value incentives, welfare, and relief. We warmly invite you to participate in this exciting and rewarding development event!

Event Details

The mechanism of SufAIthon is significantly different from traditional hackathons.

Firstly, it is not merely a competition belonging solely to a specific group; developers are expected to develop "smart commons" (a sort of public goods). This is a deepening and expansion of the decentralized application ecosystem and a contribution to the public goods sector.

Secondly, the rewards in SufAIthon are not one-off. Developers can earn rewards whenever they continuously update and upgrade a smart common to provide better value to users (development rewards supported by the Satoshi Smart Commons Development Bounty, and security rewards supported by the Satoshi Security Bounty).

Additionally, the competitive aspect of SufAIthon is less emphasized; instead, we focus more on the collaboration between people and AI. We need participants to have a holistic view of the system, promoting the transition to a new human civilization in the realm of global public initiatives.
